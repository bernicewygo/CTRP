Frequently Asked Questions

How much is the preparation fee?

We competitively offer a simple fee range of $15 to $100 for most personal tax returns. Think of it as easy, medium and hard. We also try to maintain historical fees for returning clients. Returning clients will expect to pay the same amount if their tax situations remain similar.

For most rental or small business owners, we offer a fee range of $50 to $200 for cases.

For families, there will be a discount for the second+ return.

Please contact us for a fee quote.

What services are included in the preparation fee?

We provide comprehensive audit support for your tax return. After filing your return, we provide year round service for your needs. We will help you for adjusting your return, reply to CRA reviews or filing objection to CRA assessment.

What documents will I need to bring?

Please refer to the checklist of items:

  • Information Slips
    • T4 (Employment income)
    • T4E (Employment insurance benefits)
    • T3, T5 (Interest, dividends, mutual funds)
    • T2202A (Tuition/education receipts)
    • RC62 (Universal Child Care Benefit)
    • T4A(OAS) (Old age security)
    • T4A(P) (CPP benefits)
    • T4RIF (Registered Retirement Income Fund)
    • T4RSP (RRSP Income)
    • T5007 (Social assistance payments or Workers' compensation benefits)
    • T5008 (Disposal of securities)
  • Other information slips received
    • (e.g. AGR­1, NR4, NR4(OAS), T101, T1024, T4A­NR, T4PS, T5006, T5013, T5018, US 1099­DIV/INT/B, US 1042S)
    • Income not on information slips
    • Record of income not shown on information slips, e.g. Tips not included in T4s
    • Foreign income (e.g. bank record of foreign pension income)
  • Receipts
    • RRSP contribution slip
    • Support for a child, spouse or common­law partner
    • Professional or union dues
    • Tool expenses (Tradespersons)
    • Medical expenses
    • Public transit pass receipts
    • Charitable donations
    • Political contributions
    • Child care expenses
    • Adoption expenses
    • Children's arts and sports programs
    • Interest paid on student loans
    • Carrying charges and interest expenses for investment
    • Office­-in-­home expenses
    • Exams for professional certification
    • Property Tax / Rent receipts
  • Other documentation
    • Notice of Assessment / Reassessment
    • Canada Revenue Agency correspondence
    • Sale or deemed sale of stocks, bonds or real estate
    • Rental income and expense records
    • Business income and expense records
    • Automobile / Travel logbook and expenses
    • Disability Tax Credit Certificate
    • Declaration of Conditions of Employment (T2200)
    • Volunteer Firefighters certification
